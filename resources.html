Understanding Implicit Bias

Implicit bias refers to unconscious attitudes or stereotypes that affect our understanding, actions, and decisions. These biases can influence various aspects of life, from education and hiring practices to healthcare and law enforcement.

Key Concepts

  • Implicit Bias Test (IBT): Discover how implicit biases are measured and what they reveal about our subconscious attitudes.
  • Intersectionality: Learn how overlapping social identities (race, gender, class) shape individual experiences and opportunities.
  • Real-World Effects: Understand how biases impact workplaces, education, healthcare, and justice systems.

Explore More

Here are some recommended resources to deepen your understanding and take action:

  • Harvard Implicit Association Test - Take the test and explore research on implicit bias.
  • Learning for Justice - Guides and materials for reducing bias and promoting equity.
  • American Psychological Association - Insights into how bias affects individuals and institutions.
  • National Equity Project - Practical strategies to counteract implicit bias.
  • Kirwan Institute Research - Studies on systemic inequities and their impact.
